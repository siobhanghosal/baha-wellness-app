# Clinical Recommendations

## Clinical Review Conditions

1. Do not present generic chat or free-text interfaces as if they represent finalized therapeutic or support behavior.
2. Do not present counselor or escalation routes without correct role and permission framing.
3. Do not present privacy-sensitive journeys until local persistence and role-boundary issues are addressed.
4. Add explicit labels that distinguish guidance, education, support, and escalation moments.
5. Validate the wording of risk, referral, and support content with a clinical reviewer before the next stakeholder session.

## What To Review Clinically Next

- weekly student check-in
- support escalation handoff
- teacher referral path
- parent insight and consent review
- privacy and boundaries messaging

## Clinical Success Criteria

A clinician should be able to answer yes to these questions:

- Is the support model clear and bounded?
- Are escalation paths role-appropriate?
- Are consent and privacy boundaries visible in the flow?
- Are unsupported interactions absent from the UI?
- Does the prototype avoid overclaiming clinical capability?
